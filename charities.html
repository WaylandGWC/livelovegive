Perkins School for the Blind is a famous school that helps blind children realize their potential. Some famous alumni are Helen Keller and Laura Bridgman, the first deafblind person to read. Perkins educates, trains and improves the lives of approximately 4.5 million children around the world, and provides multiple services and training programs for deaf or blind people. They also have a library with 530,000 items in braille, audio, electronic and large print formats that provide deafblind people with ways to read. You can donate to give Perkins money to provide more services and help more people, and you can volunteer at Perkins. We recommend this school because it is the oldest school for the blind in the US, and has helped many deafblind people become successful.

American Red Cross helps people recovering from world and health diasters all across America. American Red Cross is a non profit organization that was founded by Clara Barton in order to help soldiers in the civil war. Overtime, this organization spread and started helping out people in or recovering from disasters all over the world. Clara was a nurse and a very powerful woman figure in a time when women weren't as powerful, and this sends a really good message about how women can start a powerful and successful organization. You can donate money in order to help with this organization or you can volenteer and help America along the 500,000 other volenteers that help with the many diasters each year. Live Love Give reccomends this charity because this organization helps so many people and are passionate about helping people all across America. Founded for an important and unforgetting purpose, the American Red Cross shows America that helping is always the right answer.

Cradles to Crayons was founded in 2002 by Lynn Margherio, after she noticed how many outgrown or even unworn clothes her niece had. Cradles to Crayons is a charity that gives essentials to disadvantaged children, that kids shouldn't have to go without. Cradles to Crayons has offices in Chicago, Boston and Philadelphia. They donate new clothes and new toys to kids in need. You can donate money, new clothes, new toys and books, and your time by volunteering at their warehouses. Live Love Give recommends this organization because they serve over 200,000 children every year, and we believe they have a big impact on our community, and many others.

Massachusetts Department of Children and Families was founded because people in massachusetts wanted to help children who have been through hard times. DCF's 30+ locations in Massachusetts all have the same mission, helping every child find a home. DCF is a charity that helps abused and/or fostered children, find a home. DCF tries to find a home for every child through adoption help and more. DCF has 30+ locations all located across Massachusetts. You can donate money, books, clothes, toys, and more in order to support the kids that rely on DCF. Live Love Give reccomends this charity because DCF helps so many children each year. One of our team members even has adopted siblings through DCF and would highly reccomend their friendly and impacting services.

Humane Society International was founded in 2001 to help animals who were stray/struggling. They are one of the only international humane societies in the world. Help animals by encouraging going vegan, cage free animals, and very against getting rid of animals in streets, harmful shelters, and animal abuse. You can donate or be a volunteer to help animals in need. We recommend this charity because we believe that animals are living and deserve to be treated decently.

Doctors Without Borders or Médecins Sans Frontières, started in May 1968, when a group of young doctors wanted to help during conflict in Paris. DWB provides healthcare to people who do not have access to hospitals or basic medical care. DWB helps people in war zones, after natural disasters, and during epidemics. You can donate money, you can volunteer, or you can fundraise for Doctors without Borders. Live Love Give recommends this organization because they provide care to thousands of people all over the world, and they change the lives of people without access to basic medical care.
