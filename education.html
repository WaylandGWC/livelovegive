Perkins School for the Blind is a famous school that helps blind children realize their potential. Some famous alumni are Helen Keller and Laura Bridgman, the first deafblind person to read. Perkins educates, trains and improves the lives of approximately 4.5 million children around the world, and provides multiple services and training programs for deaf or blind people. They also have a library with 530,000 items in braille, audio, electronic and large print formats that provide deafblind people with ways to read. You can donate to give Perkins money to provide more services and help more people, and you can volunteer at Perkins. We recommend this school because it is the oldest school for the blind in the US, and has helped many deafblind people become successful.

I Have A Dream Foundation In 1981, Eugene M. Lang returned to the elementary school he had attended in Harlem 50 years earlier, to talk to a group of students. He was told that only a very low amount of students would graduate high school. He promised college tuition to every sixth grader who stayed in high school and graduated. After that, Lang started the "I Have a Dream Foundation". The IHDF provides emotional, educational and financial support for children and families in low-income communities, so that they have the skills and resources to pursue higher education. To help the I Have a Dream Foundation, you can volunteer, donate, or start a program. Live Love Give recommends this orginization because they make a huge impact on needy people's lives.
